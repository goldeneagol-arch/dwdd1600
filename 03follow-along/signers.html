Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776) are remembered for their later roles in U.S. history, their bold actions, or the size of their signatures. A few stand out in particular:

John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress; his large, stylish signature became iconic, so much so that “John Hancock” became slang for a signature.
Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
The primary author of the Declaration; later served as the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned diplomat, inventor, and elder statesman; helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A strong advocate for independence; later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Leader of the patriot cause, known for his role in organizing resistance such as the Boston Tea Party.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer, and the last surviving signer (died in 1832).
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only man to sign all four key American founding documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
